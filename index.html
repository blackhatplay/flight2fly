Population

Los Angeles is among the most populous city of the country. According to the recent statistics, Los Angeles County’s estimated population is 10,079,000.

Language

Los Angeles is a linguistically diverse town. At least 185 languages are spoken by the different Los Angeles County Residents. The most commonly spoken languages are English, Spanish, Chinese, Japanese, Tagalog, Korean, Vietnamese, and Farsi.

Tourist Hotspot

Los Angeles is known to break tourism records year after year. The city registered a record breaking number of visitors in the year 2019, with the figures reaching more than 50 million.

Nickname

The city is fondly called ‘the City of Angels.’ Owing to its warm and Sunny weather, it is sometimes also called as ‘the City of Flowers and Sunshine.’

Why Visit Los Angeles

Los Angeles is an iconic city, holding many distinctions under its name – it is the entertainment capital of the world (owing to Hollywood), it boasts some world-class museums, it has hosted the Summer Olympics twice, and it is a must visit place for anyone who loves beaches and warm weather.

While there is no shortage of reasons to visit this beautiful city, you must at least visit here once in your life to discover a diverse range of cuisines, festivals, and cultures that you cannot get to see anywhere else in the world.

What to see in Los Angeles

Whether you are searching for a thriving culinary scene, incredible shopping spots, outstanding museums, or fun family attractions, Los Angeles features them all!

To help you plan your touring itinerary, we have listed the top tourist attractions in the city.

1.Hollywood

If you are in Los Angeles for the first time, your trip cannot be completed without a visit to Hollywood. It is the city most popular and historic tourist destination.

It was founded in the year 1886 by H.J. Whitley, known as the “Father of Hollywood.” You can easily spend a day or two to go around the Hollywood Street.

On your visit do not forget to see the Hillside Hollywood Sign, Hollywood Boulevard, the Walk of Fame, and the Chinese Theatre.

Check out the neighborhoods such as Little Armenia, Franklin Village, Spaulding Square, Thai Town, and the Yucca Corridor.

2.Los Angeles County Museum of Art

The museum features the most amazing collections of Asian, American, Latin, and Islamic Art. There are about 130,000 exhibits, covering antiquities to the art pieces of the modern times.

3.Disneyland

Disneyland feels like a magical land, filled with princess castles and fairy tale characters. The place is dubbed as the state’s premier family vacation destination. There are adventure tides, beautiful resorts, marvelous shopping centers to take care of your entertainment at Disneyworld.

4.The Getty Center

Set on 110 acres of land, the Getty Center is a beautiful building which serves as an impressive museum. It is breathtaking in many ways as it is positioned on a hill in the Santa Monica Mountains, offering some incredible scenic views. The collection includes the European paintings, sculptures, drawings, and the 19th and 20th century photography.

5.Griffith Observatory and Griffith Park

Griffith Observatory is inside the Griffith Park, and the place is all free to the public. Take a look at the sky with the expert’s eyes as you view the moon and the planets through the Zeiss Telescope. There is also a solar telescope used for observing the sun.

Griffith Park is also home to the Los Angeles Zoo, a Greek theater, a planetarium, walking and hiking trails, and golf courses.

6.Santa Monica Pier

It is known to be the most picturesque spot in Santa Monica, offering a bevy of attractions like amusement parks, big concerts, amazing fireworks, huge holiday parties, and kitschy restaurants.

7.California Science Center

It is a perfect place to experience how do astronauts live and work in space. On visiting this place, get to explore every detail of the Space Shuttle Endeavour, allowing you to learn about the history of space travel. Other major attractions inside the museum include an IMAX theater, the largest display of LEGO art in the world, and interactive exhibits.

8.The Broad

It is an impressive museum that lays its focus on contemporary and post-war art. There are more than 2,000 works of art on display. You can visit the museum free of charge for general admission – however, you would need to book your tickets in advance for the same.

9.Venice Boardwalk

It is a famous stretch of seaside streets, featuring Muscle Beach, hard-top basketball courts, and a skate park. Feel the true vibes of the city as you stroll along the Boardwalk.

10.Peterson Automotive

It is a must visit place for the true motor enthusiasts. This car museum features more than 300 cars and motorcycles, belonging to the era of the late 19th century to the 21st century.

Take up a guided Vault your by paying some extra bucks to see some rare and unusual cars.

Where To Stay in Los Angeles

Beverly Hills – It is a plush area as it is known to be the celebrity enclave. You can book high-end hotels like the Montage, Beverly Terrace Hotel, and the Beverly Wilshire. Anaheim – If you wished to stay in the close proximity to the theme parks, find some suitable staying option in this area. The popular hotel choices in this area include the Disneyland Hotel, and Staybridge Suites Anaheim. Downtown Los Angeles – To score cheaper hotel rates, you can find yourself a hotel in Downtown Los Angeles. A popular hotel choice is Doubletree by Hilton.

What to eat in Los Angeles

The city’s culinary scene is vast, including everything from street food to the award-winning restaurants, global eateries, food trucks, and more.

Do not forget to taste a juicy cheeseburger and fries from the state’s famous burger chain, In-N-Out Burger. Enjoy the North American staple foods which include hotdogs and meat stuffed- gravy dipped sandwiches.

The dessert-lovers must definitely make their way to Apple Pie at Jones Hollywood to try out this quintessential American sweet delicacy.

Head to the wineries in Los Angeles to taste the most delicious wines produced in the state of California.

Get Out of the City

Looking for a getaway destination during your stay in Los Angeles? Make a visit to Santa Barbara which is located about 100 miles north of Los Angeles. This balmy beach town is full of towering palm trees, miles of sandy shores, and Spanish-style buildings in the neighborhood.

Explore Santa Barbara County Courthouse, Santa Barbara Botanical Garden, and Santa Barbara Zoo.
